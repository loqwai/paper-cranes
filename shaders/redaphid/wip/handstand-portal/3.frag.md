# handstand-portal/3 — Subdocta fork (build→drop arc + harder chromadepth pop)

## Origin
Forked from `handstand-portal/2` on 2026-06-13 during a live `/vibej` run (iter 17), still tuned for a **Subdocta show** (heavy dubstep/riddim). Audio via virtual mic. Fully audio-reactive — **no knobs** (all `knob_*` empty).

## Why this fork
`/2` was the 14-iteration snapshot. `/3` captures iters 15–17 added on top: the **build→drop arc** (RISER tension → DROP PUNCH release), gritty texture response, the much stronger **bass chromadepth pop**, and the treble scan sweep.

## What's new since /2
- **RISER** (iter 15) — flux + energy rising (a build) pumps a fast flicker into the rim that speeds up as it climbs; releases into DROP PUNCH. The build→drop arc.
- **RIM GRIT** (iter 16a) — high roughness × entropy buzzes the rim into dissonant flickering fragments (gnarly leads). Brightness-only, no channel split.
- **BASS POP** (iter 16b) — *user request: bass should pop the figure OUT of the screen via chromadepth.* Bass now drives the figure's depth coordinate `t→0` (reddest/nearest) ~4× harder: `bassPop = (waveletBassSpring*0.18 + waveletBassZScore*0.22 + wavelet_bassHit*0.16)*quietGate`, clamped at pure red. Through 3D glasses the dancer punches forward on every bass hit.
- **SCAN SWEEP** (iter 17) — bright treble (treble × high centroid) races a thin electric band up through the figure. Brightness-only, confined to figMask.
